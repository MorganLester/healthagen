{{ title }}

  1. HotSpot Cards clearly shows what the Case Manager should work on, centered around the Patient.
  2. Multiple Hotspots for a patient can be quickly seen without losing context.
  3. Task Bar shows what is due sorted by “Today”
  4. The User can choose to work on one of these hotspotted patients or move to another patient using the search bar or hamburger menu.
  5. The reason for the hotspot is indicated on the card. Multiple reasons are indicated by dots which can be cycled through.
  6. Clicking on the hotspot card expands to show more detail about the patient as well as the system’s rationale for being marked as a hotspot.

Patient Profile

  1. Patient Information, Tasks, Messages about the Patient, and Encounters are always available at the top of the page.
  2. If the patient is a hotpot, the system's reasoning will be highlighted.
  3. Medical Information about the patient will be represented here. If any information is a Hotspot then it will highlighted.
  4. The patient’s information is divided into the tabs along the left side of the screen. Selecting a tab will show that content on the right side of the screen.
  5. At a glance, the user can see which tabs have hotpot concerns.
  6. The current tab is identified through a full color indicator that point over to the content on the left.
  7. Hotspot items will be given additional context around why they are hot. At a glance, the user’s eye is drawn to the most critical information on the screen.
  8. The basic demographic information can be viewed at the top at any point, and will expand the top bar to show the detailed content.

Current Encounter

  1. Assessments are now non-linear. Meaning you can easily jump between assessments and to other areas of the patient profile while in an Encounter.
  2. Finding questions is much easier with type ahead search results.
  3. Problems are totaled up and addressed once the encounter has finished.
  4. The left side of the screen shows the current assessment with (1) a progress bar and (2) prompts for the next 5 questions to ask.
  5. If the conversation switches off topic, the user can type the new topic in the search bar to move to more relevant questions.
  6. If the conversation moves to medication reconciliation, the user can quickly move to the medications tab of the patient profile without stopping the assessment.
  7. The right side of the screen shows other assessments that are underway or hotspotted.
  8. Questions answered in the main assessment can also be credited to the other assessments when relevant.
  9. When a question has been answered, the set of “next questions” is updated to reflect the most relevant follow-up questions.

{{ title }}

  1. Tasks are sorted by today, Single and bulk actions are easily accessible.
  2. List is pre-filtered by the current logged in User, and more filtering can be added in simply typing the team member’s name.
  3. Task Statistics shows a roll up of common tasks and highlights patients with the most tasks associated with them.
  4. Tasks are shown in the context of a playlist that they are part (e.g., 3rd step of the weight loss playlist).
  5. Action can be taken directly via the Start button to complete the task.
  6. A toggle switch allows the user to switch from organizing tasks by time to organizing them by playlist (for batch processing).

{{ title }}

  1. Playlists are groups of tasks combined into specific flows.
  2. Each playlist has a title and a goal.
  3. The active playlists are shown on the left side of the screen. The user can identify new playlists via the search bar on the right.
  4. Patients are assigned to specific playlists based on Assessments.
  5. Playlists are Hotspots when there are tasks that are overdue or when a large number of patients are on the same task and be combined in a bulk action.
  6. A toggle switch allows the user to switch from organizing tasks by playlist to organizing them by chronological due date.

Messages

  1. Messages are discussions around patients or between Staff members and facilitate open communication in an organization.
  2. They are meant to be a replacement for both Email and Notes.
  3. Messages are placed in context of the patient or staff member. When messaging about a specific patient, that patient’s basic information appears on the right side panel.
  4. If Case Managers are entering Clinical Data then they can tag the message as such. Clinical data will be treated differently that basic team communications.
  5. Messages are automatically associated with specific patients or team members and are easily accessible and sortable thorough these tags.
